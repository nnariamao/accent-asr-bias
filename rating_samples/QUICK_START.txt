        ACCENT RATING STUDY - QUICK START GUIDE
========================================================

Welcome! Here is everything you need to get started quickly.
For full instructions, see RATING_INSTRUCTIONS.txt.


WHAT YOU ARE DOING
------------------
Listen to 24 audio clips and rate each speaker's accent strength
using a 1 to 5 scale. Takes about 30 to 45 minutes total.


RATING SCALE (use this as a quick reference)
--------------------------------------------
  1  =  Minimal accent      (near-native, very subtle)
  2  =  Slight accent       (noticeable, easy to understand)
  3  =  Moderate accent     (clearly present, still understandable)
  4  =  Strong accent       (prominent, may need extra attention)
  5  =  Very strong accent  (heavy, occasionally hard to follow)


4 STEPS TO COMPLETE YOUR RATINGS
----------------------------------
  Step 1:  Open the file "accent_rating_sheet_5raters.csv" in Excel,
           Numbers, or Google Sheets.

  Step 2:  Find your assigned column (e.g., Rater_2, Rater_3, etc.).
           Only fill in your column.

  Step 3:  For each row, open and play the audio file listed in the
           "Audio_File" column. Enter your rating (1-5) in your column.
           Replay clips as needed. Rate all 24 clips.

  Step 4:  Save your completed spreadsheet and send it back to the
           study coordinator by your assigned deadline.


REMINDERS
----------
  - Rate pronunciation only. Do not consider fluency or vocabulary.
  - Go with your honest first impression.
  - Do not discuss ratings with other raters until after submission.
  - Every row must have a rating. No blanks.
