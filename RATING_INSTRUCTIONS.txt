========================================================
   ACCENT STRENGTH RATING STUDY - INSTRUCTIONS
========================================================

Thank you for agreeing to participate in this accent strength rating study.
Your ratings will contribute to research on accent perception. Please read
these instructions carefully before you begin.


OVERVIEW
--------
You will listen to 24 short audio clips and rate the strength of the
speaker's accent on a 1 to 5 scale. Each clip features a non-native
English speaker reading a sentence aloud. Your task is to judge how
strong or noticeable their accent sounds to you.

Estimated time to complete: 30 to 45 minutes.


RATING SCALE
------------
Please use the following scale for all your ratings:

   1 = Minimal accent
       The speaker sounds near-native. Any accent present is very subtle
       and would not interfere with communication.

   2 = Slight accent
       A noticeable accent is present, but it is easy to understand the
       speaker. The accent does not significantly affect comprehension.

   3 = Moderate accent
       The speaker's accent is clearly present and identifiable. It may
       require a small amount of extra attention to follow, but the
       speaker is still easily understandable overall.

   4 = Strong accent
       There is substantial influence from the speaker's first language.
       The accent is prominent and may occasionally require extra effort
       to understand.

   5 = Very strong accent
       The accent is very heavy. The first language influence is dominant
       and the speaker may be difficult to understand at times.


STEP-BY-STEP INSTRUCTIONS
--------------------------
1. Open the rating spreadsheet (accent_rating_sheet_5raters.csv) in a
   spreadsheet program such as Excel, Numbers, or Google Sheets.

2. Locate the column assigned to your rater number (e.g., Rater_2,
   Rater_3, etc.). Do not enter ratings in any other column.

3. For each row, find the filename listed in the "Audio_File" column.
   Open and play the corresponding audio file from the shared folder.

4. Listen to the clip and decide on a rating from 1 to 5 using the
   scale above.

5. Type your rating (a single number from 1 to 5) in your assigned
   column for that row.

6. Repeat for all 24 audio clips.

7. Save the completed spreadsheet and return it to the study coordinator
   by the deadline provided.


RATING GUIDELINES
-----------------
- Focus on pronunciation and the overall impression of accent strength.
  Do not base your ratings on speech rate, fluency, or vocabulary.

- Rate your honest first impression. Try not to overthink each clip.
  Your initial reaction is what matters most.

- You may replay each audio file as many times as you need before
  deciding on a rating. There is no requirement to rate based on a
  single listen.

- Use the full range of the scale (1 through 5). Avoid clustering all
  your ratings at the middle of the scale.

- If you are unsure between two adjacent ratings (e.g., 2 or 3), pick
  whichever feels closer to your overall impression.

- Every clip must receive a rating. Do not leave any rows blank.


IMPORTANT NOTES
---------------
- Please do not discuss your ratings with other raters until after
  everyone has submitted their completed sheets. Independent ratings
  are essential for the validity of the study.

- Do not look up information about the speakers or attempt to identify
  them. Rate only what you hear.

- If you experience any technical difficulties playing the audio files,
  please contact the study coordinator before the deadline.


CONTACT
-------
If you have any questions about these instructions or the study,
please reach out to the study coordinator.


Thank you for your time and participation!
